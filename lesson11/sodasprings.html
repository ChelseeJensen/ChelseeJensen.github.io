Soda Springs Celebration

by Sarah Smith

Soda Springs has long been known for the captivating geyser found at the heart of our city. As we prepare to celebrate the early beginnings of our community we are reminded of our history. Along with our beloved geyser we have pools of carbonated water which naturally bubble. The bubbling is caused by ancient volcanic activity, When these pools were first discovered, they were actually referred to as "Beer Springs." Our citizens certainly prefer the change in name. Soda Springs is more child-friendly. Our beloved geyser erupts every hour on the hour, even in the winter. If you're unfamiliar with the smell, well, you may want to bring a bandana or something to cover your nose. The smell is quite peculiar. This year, at the end of our community celebration, don't forget to join us near the geyser for a fireworks show you will never forget. Live music, dancing, and fireworks under the night sky. Be sure to keep your distance from the geyser, getting wet at such a late hour is ill-advised. Also, be sure to collect all your trash. Fines start at $500 for littering near the geyser.
